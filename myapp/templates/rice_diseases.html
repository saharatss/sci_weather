Brown Spot Disease

Show More

Symptoms

Lesions are initially small, circular, and dark brown to purple-brown. Fully developed lesions are circular to oval with a brown to gray center, surrounded by a reddish brown margin caused by the toxin produced by the fungi. On susceptible varielesions are 5 - 14 mm long which can cause leaves to wilt. On resistant varieties, the lesions are brown and pinhead-sized.

Causal agent : Bipolaris oryzae
Percentage of damage: Seedling 10 - 60%, Grains production period 5 - 45%<

1. Monitor soil nutrients regularly and apply required fertilizers or apply calcium silicate slag before planting if silicon was
2. Use resistant cultivars.
3. Biological control such as Trichoderma spp., Bacillus subtilis, Pseudomonas fluorescens.
4. Use fungicides (e.g., iprodione, propiconazole, azoxystrobin, trifloxystrobin, and carbendazim) as seed treatments.
5. Treat seeds with hot water (53 - 54°C) for 10 - 12 minutes before planting, to control primary infection at the seedling stage.



Blast Disease

Show More

Symptoms

Initial symptoms are white to gray-green lesions or spots with darker borders produced on all parts of the shoot, while older lesions are elliptical or spindle-shaped and whitish to gray with necrotic borders. Lesions may enlarge and coalesce to kill the entire leaf. It also affects reproduction by causing the host to produce fewer seeds.

Causal agent : Magnaporthe grisea, Pyricularia oryzae (anamorph)
Percentage of damage: Seedling 90%, Grains production period 50%

Managements

1. Using resistance cultivars and seed collection by yourself.
2. Integrated management program should be implemented.
3. Managing the amount of water supplied to the crops limits spore mobility.
4. Biological control such as Trichoderma spp., Bacillus subtilis, Pseudomonas fluorescens.
5. Chemical controls such as carbendazim, kazukamycin, prochloraz, tricyclazol have been shown to prevent penetratioothe pathogen.



Bacterial Leaf Streak Disease

Show More

Symptoms

Symptoms are narrow, dark-greenish, water-soaked, interveinal streaks of various lengths, initially restricted to the leaf blades. The lesions enlarge, turn yellowish-orange to brown (depending on cultivar), and eventually coalesce. Tiny amber droplets of bacterial exudate are often present on the lesions.

Causal agent : Xanthomonas oryzae pv. oryzicola
Percentage of damage: Seedling 70%, Grains production period 30%

Managements

1. Phytosanitary measures to prevent the distribution of seed infected.
2. Healthy seed is used.
3. Suitable using nitrogenous fertilizer because the use of excess nitrogenous fertilizer has shown an increase in incidence.
4. Biological control such as Trichoderma spp., Bacillus subtilis, Pseudomonas fluorescens.
5. Other control measures are not likely to be necessary.



Dirty Panicle Disease

Show More

Symptoms

Infected seedlings have small, circular, yellow brown or brown lesions that may girdle the coleoptile and distort primary and secondary leaves.

Causal agent : Curvularia lunata, Cercospora oryzae, Bipolaris oryzae, Fusarium semitectum, Trichoconis padwickii, Sarocladium oryzae
Percentage of damage: Seedling 80%, Grains production period 40%

Managements

1. Monitor soil nutrients regularly and apply required fertilizers or apply calcium silicate slag before planting if silicon was low.
2. Use resistant cultivars.
3. Biological control such as Trichoderma spp., Bacillus subtilis, Pseudomonas fluorescens.
4. Use fungicides (e.g., iprodione, propiconazole, azoxystrobin, trifloxystrobin, and carbendazim) as seed treatments.
5. Treat seeds with hot water (53 - 54°C) for 10 - 12 minutes before planting, to control primary infection at the seedling stage.



Bacterial Leaf Blight Disease

Show More

Symptoms

Lesions are pale-green to grey-green, water-soaked streaks near the leaf tip and margins. In older plants, the leaves become yellow and then die.

Causal agent : Xanthomonas oryzae pv. oryzae
Percentage of damage: Seedling 80%, Grains production period 40%

Managements

1. Planting resistant rice cultivars.
2. Suitable using nitrogenous fertilizer because the use of excess nitrogenous fertilizer has shown an increase in incidence.
3. Biological control such as Trichoderma spp., Bacillus subtilis, Pseudomonas fluorescens.
4. Chemical controls such as Carbendazim, kazukamycin, prochloraz, tricyclazol have been shown to prevent penetration of the pathogen.
